# Understanding Training Stress Balance TSB through Aerobic Efficiency

## 1. Get Your Numbers Right: The Athlete's Execution Plan
Forget guessing. If you want to stand on the top step of the podium, you must train by the numbers. **Training Stress Balance TSB** is the core operational scorecard for your season. By focusing on **Aerobic Efficiency**, you will systematically build your aerobic engine and manage fatigue to hit your peak power right when the starting gun fires.

For our altitude blocks in St. Moritz or Sierra Nevada, the directive is clear: track your adaptation metrics daily. We use this data to monitor your erythropoietin (EPO) stimulation, verify blood plasma expansion, and control metabolic decoupling. Do not let your intensity drift; execute the prescription exactly to secure your supercompensation for race day.

## 2. The Prescription: Load Decay and Calculations
Calculate your training response daily. To track how your body adapts to the work, apply these formulas to your training software:

$$ \text{ATL}_t = \text{ATL}_{t-1} \cdot e^{-1/7} + \text{TSS} \cdot (1 - e^{-1/7}) $$

Follow these baseline rules:
*   $\text{CTL}_t$ and $\text{ATL}_t$ represent your Chronic and Acute Training Load. Think of CTL as your fitness bank built over a 42-day cycle, and ATL as your fatigue penalty over a 7-day window. Keep these values balanced.
*   $\text{TSB}_t$ represents your freshness. Do not race when your TSB is deep in the negative. Taper your volume until TSB rises above zero to unlock your peak performance.
*   $VO_2$ is your maximum oxygen consumption, computed directly from your ventilation volumes ($V_E$) and oxygen extraction rates.

## 3. Coach's Action Plan: Field Protocols
Implement these three training protocols to upgrade your **Aerobic Efficiency**:
1.  **VLaMax Anaerobic Capacity Management**: Suppress excess glycolytic capacity. Perform low-cadence, high-torque efforts (40-50 rpm) on steep grades to force muscular adaptation and reduce your VLaMax, allowing you to spare glycogen for the final sprint.
2.  **Heart Rate Decoupling**: Monitor your aerobic foundation. Ride at a steady Zone 2 power for 4-5 hours and check the separation between heart rate and power. If decoupling exceeds 5%, your cardiovascular endurance needs work.
3.  **W' Reconstitution Dynamics**: Manage your matchbook. Use real-time modeling of your $W'$ recharge rates to pace your time trials and regulate recovery intervals between hard climbs. Do not burn your anaerobic reserve too early.
