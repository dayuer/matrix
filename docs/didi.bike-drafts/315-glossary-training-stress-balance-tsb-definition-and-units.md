# Training Stress Balance TSB Definition and Units

## Step 1: Definition of Physical Stress Indicators
In professional team workshops, Training Stress Balance TSB serves as a critical diagnostic metric. This value measures the delta between fitness and fatigue. To obtain clean inputs, mechanics must ensure the drivetrain exhibits minimal friction losses. When calibrating the power meter to zero out the temperature-induced offset, technicians perform a calibration offset routine that ensures the torque tolerance remains within one percent of the nominal value. Wrenches adjust bolts. Slop ruins accuracy. We center sensors.

## Step 2: Drivetrain Component Preparation
Before applying the thread lock compound to the chainring bolts, the mechanic must verify that the strain gauge centering aligns perfectly with the crank arm axis to minimize friction losses. Mechanics must execute a thorough play/slop detection check. Loose chainrings distort the stress score outputs. Clean the threads. Tighten each screw. Carbon cranks demand high precision. Environmental sealing preserves internal components from moisture ingress.

## Step 3: Verifying the Mathematical Formulation
We calculate physical strain using power telemetry streams. The standard mathematical relation is modeled as:

$$ \text{TSS} = \frac{t \cdot P \cdot \text{IF}}{3600 \cdot \text{FTP}} \cdot 100 $$

Where:
*   $\text{TSS}$ and $\text{NP}$ reflect the exponential weighting of training stress, scaling with the 4th power of mechanical power output to match physiological load.
*   $RER$ represents the Respiratory Exchange Ratio, indicating substrate oxidation ratios (carbohydrate vs. fat combustion).
*   $W'_{t}$ represents the instantaneous anaerobic work capacity remaining, measured in Joules (J), which drains non-linearly above FTP and reconstitutes exponentially during recovery.

Evaluating this output helps coaches monitor systemic exhaustion. Check the chain. Gears shift smoothly.
