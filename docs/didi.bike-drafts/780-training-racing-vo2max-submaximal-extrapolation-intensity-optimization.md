# Understanding VO2max Submaximal Extrapolation through Intensity Optimization

## 1. Addressing the Friction of Traditional Athletic Testing
For serious endurance athletes and coaching organizations, executing traditional laboratory VO2max assessments presents significant friction: they are invasive, expensive, and logistically complex to repeat. To resolve these pain points, we utilize **VO2max Submaximal Extrapolation**. This software-driven analysis acts as a non-invasive feature that evaluates everyday riding metrics to map aerobic capacity. Through **Intensity Optimization**, users receive automated training zone updates without the need for frequent exhaustive testing.

In key use cases, such as high-altitude team camps in St. Moritz or Sierra Nevada, the algorithm tracks physiological adaptions in real-time. By monitoring markers of erythropoietin (EPO) activity, blood plasma expansion, and metabolic decoupling, the system evaluates altitude adaptation success, helping users plan their taper to deliver peak supercompensation on competition day.

## 2. System Architecture: The Readiness Dashboard
The processing engine translates training data into three high-value metrics, calculating long-term preparation against acute recovery debt:

$$ \text{TSB}_t = \text{CTL}_{t-1} - \text{ATL}_{t-1} $$

Where:
*   $\text{CTL}_t$ (Chronic Training Load) functions as a 42-day moving average, tracking accumulated fitness assets.
*   $\text{ATL}_t$ (Acute Training Load) serves as a 7-day moving average, representing the athlete's short-term stress debt.
*   $\text{TSB}_t$ (Training Stress Balance) is the primary readiness KPI, indicating optimal performance windows when the metric trends positive.
*   $VO_2$ is the core metabolic input, calculated by measuring the rate of oxygen extracted from inspired volume ($V_E$) during respiration.

## 3. Product Features and Value Delivery
Implementing **Intensity Optimization** delivers three high-utility features to the user’s dashboard:
1.  **VLaMax Anaerobic Capacity Management Module**: This feature guides glycogen conservation. By prompting the user to perform specific low-cadence torque intervals, the platform helps lower carbohydrate combustion rates, preserving energy reserves for late-stage sprints.
2.  **Heart Rate Decoupling Alert**: A diagnostic utility that flags cardiac drift. When heart rate diverges from mechanical power during long rides, the system alerts the rider to potential dehydration or thermal stress.
3.  **W' Reconstitution Tracker**: A real-time pacing battery indicator. By modeling the recharge rate of the anaerobic energy reserve ($W'$), riders can avoid premature exhaustion on multi-climb courses.
