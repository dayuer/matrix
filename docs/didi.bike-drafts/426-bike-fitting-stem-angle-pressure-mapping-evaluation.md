# Saddle Pressure Optimization for Sustained Target Wattage

## Address the Usability Barrier on the Saddle
To maintain your target wattage during intensive threshold training blocks, you must ensure that your interface setup is perfectly stable. Comfort increases output. While a low cockpit geometry helps decrease frontal area, it can also cause excessive saddle pressure spikes that force you to rock your pelvis under load. Rocking wastes power. By optimizing handlebar coordinates through precise adjustments, you can support positive training adaptations and avoid premature fatigue accumulation. Pedal with purpose. Use the workout outline below to build your aerobic engine and test your position dynamically.

## Prescribing the Interval Block
Table 1 lists the target intensity zones for your dynamic assessment.

| Workout Phase | Target Intensity Zone | Block Duration (min) | Target Cadence Range (rpm) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Active Warm-Up | Zone 2 (Aerobic Endurance) | 15 | 90 - 95 |
| Threshold Work | Zone 4 (FTP Threshold) | 3 x 10 (Recovery 5) | 85 - 90 |
| Active Cool-Down | Zone 1 (Active Recovery) | 10 | 90 - 100 |

During the high-torque segments, pay close attention to your contact points. If you feel excessive pressure on the nose of the saddle, adjust your positioning. Dynamic saddle stability leads to glycogen sparing by reducing upper body tension.

## Biomechanical Math Validation
We use mathematical joint models to check your extension profile. Equations determine leverage. The cosine rule models dynamic knee tracking:

$$ \theta_{\text{knee}} = \arccos\left( \frac{a^2 + b^2 - c^2}{2ab} \right) $$

Where:
*   $L_{\text{saddle}}$ is the saddle height calculated via the Lemond or 109% inseam formulas, serving as the baseline for joint flexion.
*   $\theta_{\text{knee}}$ is the dynamic knee angle, modeled using the cosine rule where $a$, $b$, and $c$ represent the femur length, tibia length, and effective seat height.
*   $F_{\text{joint}}$ represents the shear force acting on the knee joint as a function of the pedaling force and joint extension angles.

## Executing the Training Plan
Focus on your pelvic orientation when pushing through high resistance intervals. Keeping your lumbar spine in a neutral alignment ensures maximum gluteal engagement. This improves your lactate clearance capacity over time. Stay focused, complete the work, and analyze the telemetry data from your power meter to track long-term improvements.
