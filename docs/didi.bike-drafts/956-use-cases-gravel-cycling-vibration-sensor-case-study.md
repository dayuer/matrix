# Understanding Gravel Cycling Vibration Sensor through Case Study

## 1. Case Study & Engineering Application
Gravel is not just dirt; it is a relentless fight against cumulative fatigue. Every high-frequency vibration through the handlebars drains your energy long before the final sprint. To understand this physical toll, we took telemetry tools out of the lab and onto the road. This case study details how we put the gravel cycling vibration sensor to work under demanding conditions. By gathering raw sensor metrics, we analyzed frame dampening and real-world forces to help riders conquer rough terrain.

For professional teams like Tudor Pro Cycling, these methods help optimize team time trial alignments. By tracking wind speed telemetry with dual sensors, directors can position riders to decrease collective aerodynamic resistance.

## 2. Mechanical Power and Vibration Physics
To track how much energy gets lost to the washboard roads, we use first-principles physical equations to model the forces at play:

$$ a_{\text{vibration}} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{T} \int_0^T [a(t)]^2 \, dt} $$

Where:
*   $P_{\text{total}}$ represents the total mechanical power output required to overcome gravity, aerodynamic drag, rolling resistance, and drivetrain friction.
*   $a_{\text{vibration}}$ is the root-mean-square acceleration of frame vibration, measured via triaxial accelerometers to evaluate dampening characteristics.
*   $\eta_{\text{drafting}}$ represents the drafting efficiency coefficient, representing the percentage reduction in CdA when riding in a slipstream.

## 3. Practical Field Implementations & Case Study
Moving from equations to the dirt, field testing gives us concrete data to make key adjustments:
1.  **Suspension Telemetry Validation**: Mounting linear potentiometers on suspension forks let us track compression and rebound rates on rough trails, helping us dial in traction for fast descents.
2.  **Chung Virtual Elevation Field Protocols**: Riding constant-power loops on outdoor roads allowed us to calculate aerodynamic CdA with $\pm 1.5\%$ precision without using a wind tunnel.
3.  **Pedal Stroke Optimization**: Tracking pedal force vectors dynamically during fitting sessions showed us exactly how to adjust cleat alignment, resolving knee discomfort.
