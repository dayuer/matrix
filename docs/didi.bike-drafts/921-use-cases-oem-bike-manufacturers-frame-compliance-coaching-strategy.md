## 1. Athlete Preparation and Mechanical Alignment
As coaches, our role is to align human physiology with mechanical efficiency. An athlete's training load cannot be separated from the surface vibrations and resistance they face on the road. When structuring macrocycles for elite road racing, we implement a targeted **Coaching Strategy** that connects **OEM Bike Manufacturers Frame Compliance** directly to physical recovery and performance longevity.

$$ \eta_{\text{drafting}} = 1 - \frac{CdA_{\text{drafted}}}{CdA_{\text{solo}}} $$

## 3. Applying Field Data to Training Plans
Integrating field measurements into our coaching protocols leads to direct adaptations:
1.  **Suspension Monitoring**: Tracking linear potentiometers on mountain bike forks helps us analyze rebound speed under race loads, ensuring the rider retains control and reduces forearm fatigue on technical descents.
2.  **CdA Profiling**: Using outdoor constant-power loops and the Chung virtual elevation protocol allows triathletes to measure aerodynamic CdA with $\pm 1.5\%$ accuracy, helping us verify setup aerodynamic improvements.
3.  **Biomechanical Fitting**: Monitoring dynamic pedal force vectors on fitting bikes lets us adjust shoe cleat alignment on the fly, preventing repetitive motion strain during return-to-play phases post-surgery.
