As mechanics, we calibrate the machinery before it hits the road, ensuring every sensor reads clean signals. ## 3. Practical Calibration & Field Protocol
Executing a precise **Field Protocol** on active equipment isolates mechanical errors and yields real-world improvements:
1.  **Fork Calibration**: Mounting linear potentiometers on mountain bike forks allows us to record compression and rebound behaviors, giving suspension technicians the data needed to maximize grip.
2.  **Aerodynamic Verification**: By tracking steady-state loops on local roads with onboard power sensors and calculating virtual elevation using the Chung protocol, we can map CdA variations within $\pm 1.5\%$ without wind tunnel access.
3.  **Contact Point Adjustments**: We measure active pedal force vectors on dedicated fit systems, adjusting shoe cleats to prevent joint strain and optimize the rider's leg alignment.
