# Understanding Frame Aerodynamic Testing Velodrome through Coaching Strategy

## 1. Case Study & Engineering Application
Every watt saved is energy reserved for the final sprint. In frame aerodynamic testing velodrome, our coaching goal is to turn technical data into winning behaviors on the track. By implementing a clear coaching strategy, we help athletes adapt their riding positions and pacing to make full use of their aerodynamic setup under race conditions.

When preparing Swiss-based Tudor Pro Cycling for team time trials, we use dual-sensor wind speed telemetry to teach drafting discipline. We instruct riders on the exact distance to maintain from the wheel ahead. This training allows the team to decrease cumulative group aerodynamic resistance and maintain high speeds longer.

## 2. Mechanical Power and Vibration Physics
We teach our riders to understand the efficiency of their position in the paceline:

$$ \eta_{\text{drafting}} = 1 - \frac{CdA_{\text{drafted}}}{CdA_{\text{solo}}} $$

Where:
*   $P_{\text{total}}$ represents the total mechanical power output required to overcome gravity, aerodynamic drag, rolling resistance, and drivetrain friction.
*   $a_{\text{vibration}}$ is the root-mean-square acceleration of frame vibration, measured via triaxial accelerometers to evaluate dampening characteristics.
*   $\eta_{\text{drafting}}$ represents the drafting efficiency coefficient, representing the percentage reduction in CdA when riding in a slipstream.

Our job is to help riders maintain their position even when fatigue sets in.

## 3. Practical Field Implementations & Coaching Strategy
We apply these coaching strategies across different training scenarios:
1.  **Suspension Telemetry Validation**: We use data from linear potentiometers on mountain bike forks to teach riders how compression and rebound affect traction, building confidence on rough descents.
2.  **Chung Virtual Elevation Field Protocols**: Triathletes practice constant-power loops on outdoor roads. This protocol allows them to calculate aerodynamic CdA with $\pm 1.5\%$ precision and learn to hold their aero position.
3.  **Pedal Stroke Optimization**: Tracking pedal force vectors on fit bikes helps us guide athletes in adjusting their cleat alignment, resolving post-surgery knee strain.
