Understanding W-Prime Depletion and Reconstitution through Aerobic Efficiency

1. Coaching the Recovery Rate of the Anaerobic Engine

To win road races, you cannot rely solely on the size of your anaerobic battery; you must train your body to recharge it as quickly as possible. The capacity to repeat high-power efforts above threshold—what we define as W-Prime Depletion and Reconstitution—is heavily dependent on your Aerobic Efficiency. The stronger your aerobic base, the faster your body can clear metabolic byproducts and rebuild your anaerobic reserves ($W'$, measured in Joules) during recovery phases.

During our altitude blocks in St. Moritz or Sierra Nevada, the coaching staff monitors your biological markers to evaluate hypoxic adaptation. We track how well your system stimulates erythropoietin (EPO) to build red blood cells, expands blood plasma volume, and handles metabolic strain. By adjusting your load based on these adaptations, we time your taper to ensure you achieve supercompensation just in time for the race.

2. Cardiorespiratory Balancing: Monitoring Fitness and Fatigue

To build your intervals and plan your recovery blocks, we track cardiorespiratory variables using standard metrics:

VO2=VE⋅(FIO2−FEO2)VO_2 = V_E \cdot (F_I O_2 - F_E O_2)VO2​=VE​⋅(FI​O2​−FE​O2​)

Where:

  • $VO_2$ is your metabolic oxygen consumption rate, derived from minute expiratory ventilation ($V_E$) and oxygen gas fractions.
  • $\text{CTL}_t$ (Chronic Training Load) and $\text{ATL}_t$ (Acute Training Load) represent the accumulation of training stress over 42-day and 7-day periods.
  • $\text{TSB}_t$ (Training Stress Balance) tracks freshness, indicating the optimal time to launch maximum-effort workouts.

3. Practical Drills and Road Coaching Cues

To train your body to depletion and recharge $W'$ under stress, we execute three key drills:

  1. VLaMax Management (Low-Cadence Torque Drills): Perform low-cadence efforts (e.g., 4x8 minutes at Zone 3, holding 50-55 RPM) to lower your glycolytic rate (VLaMax). This changes how your body burns fuel, conserving muscle glycogen for late-stage sprints.
  2. Cardiovascular Decoupling Test (The Efficiency Audit): During prolonged endurance rides, watch for cardiac drift, where your heart rate climbs while power output remains flat. If you detect decoupling, focus on hydration to support blood plasma volume.
  3. W' Reconstitution Intervals (Over-Under Drills): We use over-under intervals to train recharge dynamics. Ride for 2 minutes at 115% of Critical Power to deplete $W'$, followed immediately by 3 minutes at 90% of Critical Power. This forces your body to clear lactate and recover anaerobic Joules under mechanical load.
