Understanding Gravel Cycling Vibration Sensor through Optimization Workflow

1. Case Study & Engineering Application

Building sports technology is not just about collecting raw data; it is about making that data actionable for the end user. When developing the gravel cycling vibration sensor, our product goal was to transform complex raw metrics into clear setup recommendations. This optimization workflow defines how we guide coaches, mechanics, and riders through a feedback loop that translates high-frequency vibrations into real-world comfort and speed.

For elite clients like Tudor Pro Cycling, our product workflow streamlines high-stakes decisions. When optimizing team time trial dynamics, directors use our integrated dashboard to quickly adjust rider pacing order based on real-time aerodynamic telemetry.

3. Practical Field Implementations & Optimization Workflow

We structured our product optimization workflow to solve common riding problems with clear, step-by-step actions:

  1. Suspension Telemetry Validation: By mapping fork potentiometer logs onto a user-friendly setup wizard, riders receive immediate pressure and damping suggestions to maximize off-road traction.
  2. Chung Virtual Elevation Field Protocols: Our software guides triathletes through standardized road tests, calculating aerodynamic CdA with $\pm 1.5%$ accuracy without requiring laboratory testing.
  3. Pedal Stroke Optimization: The system visualizes force vectors dynamically, giving fitters clear hints to adjust cleat positions and resolve post-injury knee strain.
