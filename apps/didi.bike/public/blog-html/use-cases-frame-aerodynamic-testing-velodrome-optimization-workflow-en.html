Understanding Frame Aerodynamic Testing Velodrome through Optimization Workflow

1. Case Study & Engineering Application

Optimizing sports performance requires a structured, repeatable process. In frame aerodynamic testing velodrome, our priority is to streamline the testing workflow to maximize track time efficiency. By standardizing the optimization workflow, we eliminate bottlenecks in data collection, ensuring that engineers, mechanics, and athletes work in alignment to improve aerodynamic profiles.

When managing tests for Swiss-based Tudor Pro Cycling during team time trial preparations, we coordinate the setup of dual-sensor wind speed telemetry. We focus on shortening the feedback loop between runs, allowing directors to test multiple rider arrangements quickly and find the lineup that minimizes total group drag.

2. Mechanical Power and Vibration Physics

Our systemic approach maps all energy losses into a unified performance framework:

Ptotal=Pgravity+Paero+Prr+PdrivetrainP_{\text{total}} = P_{\text{gravity}} + P_{\text{aero}} + P_{\text{rr}} + P_{\text{drivetrain}}Ptotal​=Pgravity​+Paero​+Prr​+Pdrivetrain​

Where:

  • $P_{\text{total}}$ represents the total mechanical power output required to overcome gravity, aerodynamic drag, rolling resistance, and drivetrain friction.
  • $a_{\text{vibration}}$ is the root-mean-square acceleration of frame vibration, measured via triaxial accelerometers to evaluate dampening characteristics.
  • $\eta_{\text{drafting}}$ represents the drafting efficiency coefficient, representing the percentage reduction in CdA when riding in a slipstream.

Understanding how these sub-systems interact allows us to prioritize engineering efforts.

3. Practical Field Implementations & Optimization Workflow

We apply this structured optimization workflow to resolve user pain points:

  1. Suspension Telemetry Validation: Utilizing linear potentiometers on mountain bike forks simplifies the tuning process, helping suspension engineers iterate compression and rebound settings to improve traction on rough descents.
  2. Chung Virtual Elevation Field Protocols: Implementing constant-power loops on outdoor roads provides triathletes with an accessible method to calculate aerodynamic CdA with $\pm 1.5%$ precision without wind tunnel costs.
  3. Pedal Stroke Optimization: Tracking pedal force vectors dynamically on commercial fit bikes gives fitters an objective tool to modify cleat alignment and address post-surgery knee strain.
