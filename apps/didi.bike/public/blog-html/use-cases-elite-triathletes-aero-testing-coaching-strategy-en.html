Coaching Triathletes for Aerodynamic Success: A Practical Playbook

You must balance your athlete's training hours. Aero testing should not compromise key threshold workouts or accumulate excessive fatigue. Planning is everything.

Prescribing the Aero Adaptation Workout

Schedule testing sessions during base and early build cycles. This timing ensures your athlete has ample opportunity for training adaptations. Maintain target wattage consistency during your sessions to ensure clean telemetry inputs. Verify the setup.

Training Phase and Workout Specifications

Training Phase Workout Target Focus Duration & Prescribed Intensity Cadence Target
Base period Position familiarization 2x20 min at 85% FTP (Zone 3) 90–95 rpm
Build period Aero interval adaptation 4x8 min at 105% FTP (Zone 4) 85–90 rpm
Pre-race Race pace telemetry validation 60 min at 95% FTP (Zone 3/4) 90 rpm
Race week Locked-in posture tapering 15 min at 80% FTP (Zone 2) 90–95 rpm

Run your tests on consecutive days. Keep wind variables low.

Executing the Test Protocol

Brief your athlete clearly:

"You will maintain your target wattage on a 4 km loop. We will run this sequence repeatedly. I will adjust one setting per block. Focus on keeping your cadence steady and your torso relaxed."

Maintaining a steady pace prevents signal noise. Let the telemetry hardware log the run.

Translating Drag Data Into Time Savings

Show your athletes how drag area reductions improve velocity. Translate the drag area coefficient ($CdA$) into seconds saved:

Explain: "We reduced your CdA from 0.268 to 0.245. At your target wattage of 250 W, you save 1.8 minutes over 90 km. This moves you up several places."

Monitoring Neuromuscular and Metabolic Adaptations

Watch for these indicators as your athlete adapts to the optimized cockpit over a three-week window:

  1. Power stability: Confirm your athlete holds their target wattage for prolonged periods. A power drop greater than 3% indicates an excessively aggressive setup.
  2. Cardiorespiratory strain: Restricted diaphragm space limits lactate clearance capacity. Check for heart rate decoupling at threshold.
  3. Run-off quality: Check hip flexor tightness during transition runs. If running mechanics degrade, raise the stack.
  4. Fatigue accumulation: Track comfort scores. Adaptations to position changes take 7 to 10 rides.

Focus on building a robust aerobic engine. A fast position is useless if it compromises glycogen sparing. Comfort enables power.
