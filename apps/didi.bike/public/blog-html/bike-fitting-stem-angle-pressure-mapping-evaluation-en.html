Saddle Pressure Optimization for Sustained Target Wattage

Address the Usability Barrier on the Saddle

To maintain your target wattage during intensive threshold training blocks, you must ensure that your interface setup is perfectly stable. Comfort increases output. While a low cockpit geometry helps decrease frontal area, it can also cause excessive saddle pressure spikes that force you to rock your pelvis under load. Rocking wastes power. By optimizing handlebar coordinates through precise adjustments, you can support positive training adaptations and avoid premature fatigue accumulation. Pedal with purpose. Use the workout outline below to build your aerobic engine and test your position dynamically.

Executing the Training Plan

Focus on your pelvic orientation when pushing through high resistance intervals. Keeping your lumbar spine in a neutral alignment ensures maximum gluteal engagement. This improves your lactate clearance capacity over time. Stay focused, complete the work, and analyze the telemetry data from your power meter to track long-term improvements.
