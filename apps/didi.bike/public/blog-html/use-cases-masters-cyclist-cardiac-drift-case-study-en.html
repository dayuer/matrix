Understanding Masters Cyclist Cardiac Drift through Case Study

1. Case Study & Engineering Application

As a masters cyclist, I have realized that my body does not react the way it did in my twenties. On long, hot endurance rides, I often watch my heart rate climb even when my power output stays perfectly flat—a phenomenon known as masters cyclist cardiac drift. Understanding this cardiovascular fatigue is the difference between blowing up and pacing a perfect race. In this case study, we examine how senior racers use telemetry tools to monitor cardiac load, analyzing real-world metrics to manage our efforts and sustain power over multi-hour endurance events.

Even for professional structures like Tudor Pro Cycling, cardiovascular tracking is a cornerstone of training. By monitoring drafting coefficients and wind telemetry, team directors can place riders where they experience minimal heart rate spikes and muscle strain.

3. Practical Field Implementations & Case Study

Fusing cardiovascular tracking with bike setup telemetry helped me solve my pacing issues on long rides:

  1. Suspension Telemetry Validation: Tuning my suspension via fork potentiometers reduced front-end road buzz, lowering my upper body fatigue and keeping my heart rate stable on technical terrain.
  2. Chung Virtual Elevation Field Protocols: Riding constant-power loops let me calculate my aerodynamic footprint within a $\pm 1.5%$ margin, helping me find a sustainable, low-drag posture.
  3. Pedal Stroke Optimization: Fusing pedal force vectors showed me where my stroke was uneven, allowing me to adjust my cleats and resolve knee strain.
