Prescribing the Peaking Phase

As you approach your target race, you must carefully calculate fatigue accumulation. Your lactate clearance capacity determines how quickly you can recover from high-wattage surges. During high-intensity blocks, you should target wattage zones that stimulate positive physiological shifts without overwhelming your recovery capacity. If a rider pushes deep into negative balance during high-altitude base training, their glycogen sparing capacity drops rapidly, leading to a complete depletion of their muscle energy stores before the target competition. When planning your next block, keep your target wattage targets within realistic limits. Adjust your intensity based on environmental stress, especially during extreme heat or high altitude.
