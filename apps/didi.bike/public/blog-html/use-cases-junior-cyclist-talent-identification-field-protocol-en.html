Understanding Junior Cyclist Talent Identification through Field Protocol

1. Case Study & Engineering Application

Standardizing equipment telemetry is essential when assessing development-level riders to ensure that differences in data reflect physical capability rather than hardware variance. In junior cyclist talent identification, technicians must follow strict protocols for mounting sensors, calibrating power meters, and verifying communication lines. This field protocol details the setup procedures needed to gather clean telemetry for evaluating young athletes' aerodynamic efficiency, frame interaction, and biomechanical outputs.

For professional structures like Tudor Pro Cycling's development program, standardization is vital. When evaluating draft potential, technicians must match sensor heights and calibrate dual wind telemetry systems to accurately measure relative wind angles across all bikes.

3. Practical Field Implementations & Field Protocol

Implementing these structured field protocols allows technicians to collect accurate performance baselines:

  1. Suspension Telemetry Validation: Connect linear potentiometers to the fork stanchions. Mechanics verify compression and rebound travel ranges to assess the junior rider’s bike control on rough descents.
  2. Chung Virtual Elevation Field Protocols: Run riders through steady constant-power loops, logging power meter and barometric variables to calculate aerodynamic CdA with $\pm 1.5%$ precision.
  3. Pedal Stroke Optimization: Mount load-sensing pedal spindles to map dynamic force vectors, providing fitters with the metrics required to adjust cleat positions and prevent injury.
